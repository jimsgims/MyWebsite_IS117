What's so important about your online data?

The Internet is a wonderful place with unlimited amounts of content and websites for people to utilize for a multitude of purposes. With it though, every individual carries with them digital data that includes information about themselves. This information isn't just things like your basic information like your age or your social security number, but your habits and your interests. Websites don't need you to tell them directly what you're interested in, but instead utilize algorithms to learn more about you.

This is the concept behind the need for internet privacy; users like yourself are constantly being invaded by the sites you use, your internet service provider, your government, and any services you use on your computer. Companies make money by selling your digital data off to third-party marketers. Furthermore, there is fear of doxxing, or the reveal of your personal information by posting it online with malicious intent.

This website goes into detail how you're being tracked online, measures you can take to preventt your online information being stolen and used, and some history lessons around online privacy.
