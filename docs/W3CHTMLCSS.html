W3C, HTML and CSS

What is the W3C?

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) is an international community where they work to develop standards for the Internet. It is currently led by Web-inventor Tim Berners-Lee and CEO Jeffrey Jaffe. Their mission is to develop protocols and guidelines to ensure the long-term growth of the Internet. Here are a few of their goals:

  • Web for All - have the benefits of human communication, commerce, and opportunities to share knowledge available to all people, regardless of any limitations that people are experiencing.
  • Web on Everything - have the Internet available to all electronic devices.
  • Web for Rich Interaction - encourage the development of new ways of creating a flourishing market for content and personalized Web experiences.
  • Web of Data and Services - create an environment where the Internet is both a giant repository of linked data while also a giant set of services that exchange messages.
  • Web of Trust - encourage the new and unprecedented nature of social relationships developed on the Internet.

    • Tim Berners-Lee

    The father of the World Wide Web. Current director of W3C.

    Wikipedia

    Tim Berners-Lee is a computer scientists best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. He is known for this as he implemented the first successful communication between an HTTP client and server via the Internet in November of 1989.

    Amongst other titles, he's a professorial fellow of Computer Science at the University of Oxford and a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). He's won many awards and honours, such as being knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in 2004 and the ACM Turing Award in 2016.

    HTML

    HTML is the language used for describing the structure of web pages, allowing authors to:

  • Publish online documents with headings, text, tables, lists, photos, etc.
  • Retrieve online information via hypertext links at the click of a button
  • Design forms for conducing transactions with remote services, for use in searching for information, making reservations, ordering products, and more
  • Include spreadsheets, video clips, sound clips, and other applications directly in their documents.

    • CSS

    CSS is the language used for describing the presentation of web pages, including colors, layouts, and fonts. It helps us adapt the presentation to different types of devices with small and large screens. It is independent of HTML and can be used with any XML-based markup language; this makes it easier to maintain sites, share style sheets across pages, and tailor pages to different environments.
